Phi Delta Theta

See more

The historic Washington Alpha chapter of Phi Delta Theta was founded in 1901, and is one of the oldest social Fraternities on campus. Phi Delta Theta has set the standard for fraternal excellence at UW for over 100 years, fostering an elite environment for the cultivation of gentlemen, scholars and athletes. Numerous Olympic, NBA and NFL athletes, Fortune 500 company founders and civic leaders across the nation have passed through the doors of the historic chapter house at 2111 NE 47th St. A few famous Phis from the University of Washington chapter include Tom Tully O’keefe (founder of Tully's Coffee), Aber Whitcomb (co-founder of Myspace), Detlef Schrempf (famous basketball player for the Huskies and the Seattle SuperSonics), William Kirschner (invented the modern fiberglass ski and founded the K2 corporation), Booth Gardner (Governor of the State of Washington, responsible for the running start program in WA), Jack Medica (Olympic swimmer - Gold Medalist), and Michael Callahan (head coach of the Husky rowing team, World Rowing Championship Gold Medalist, Olympic Rower). The opportunity to become a member of Phi Delta Theta allows students to develop a positive fraternity experience and work towards establishing a great reputation on campus. Becoming a member of Phi Delt at UW also provides a professional network of more than 160,000 alumni living throughout North America, and over 3,000 in the Seattle area. For many of Phi Delta Theta's members, their experience allows them to hone their personal leadership skills and prepare for life after college. In the coming school year, Phi Delta Theta offers a unique opportunity for young leaders to follow in the footsteps of Jack Medica, William Kirschner, and nearly 2,500 UW alumni to answer the question: Are You a Leader? The Phi Delta Theta Washington Alpha Chapter is pleased to announce that it is offering $20,000 in scholarships this Summer. Outstanding gentlemen who will be starting their academic careers at the University of Washington during the 2014­-2015 academic year are eligible to apply. All students are eligible to receive an award, not only those who plan on joining a Fraternity!

Phi Psi

See more

The University of Washington Chapter of Phi Kappa Psi has a long-standing tradition here on campus. We maintain a well established brotherhood and presence on campus since we were locally founded in 1914. Phi Psi provides more than just a place to live; it provides you with experiences that will shape the rest of your life. We keep a full social calendar, excel in every type of intramural sports, constantly give back to the community through the local Boys and Girls Club, and on top of that maintain a high standard for academic success. We place heavy emphasis on leadership development, offering a wide variety of leadership positions within the house while also supporting involvment on campus and in clubs. Twice a year, our chapter attends the American Leadership Academy in Cabo San Lucas, which is an opportunity unique to Phi Kappa Psi. The cost of the trip is heavily subsidized by alumni and scholarship support, giving anyone the opportunity to attend. This is one of many testaments to our unmatched alumni network and demonstrates how strongly they are involved with our undergraduates. Our scholarship fund is the fourth largest within Phi Kappa Psi nationally, and one of the largest in the UW Greek Community. This endowment fund allows our chapter to award over $21,000 annually to brothers living in the house. We also have a large diversity of interests within our membership, ranging from UW athletes to engineers and musicians. We strive to maintain the perfect balance of academic and social excellence.
